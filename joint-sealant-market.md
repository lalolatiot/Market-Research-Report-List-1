<p><p>The joint sealant market includes various types, prominently featuring polyurethane and inorganic joint sealants. Polyurethane joint sealants are known for their flexibility, durability, and strong adhesion, making them ideal for dynamic joints in construction and industrial applications. In contrast, inorganic joint sealants, often silicone or silicate-based, offer superior resistance to extreme temperatures and environmental conditions. They are suitable for static joints and areas requiring high chemical resistance, such as infrastructure projects and heavy-duty construction. Both types cater to diverse sealing needs, enhancing structural integrity.</p></p>
