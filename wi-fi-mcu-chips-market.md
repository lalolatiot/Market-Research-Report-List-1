<p><p>The Wi-Fi MCU chip market is segmented into two main types: single band and dual band. Single band chips operate on one frequency, typically 2.4 GHz, making them cost-effective for basic applications. In contrast, dual band chips support both 2.4 GHz and 5 GHz frequencies, offering better performance, reduced interference, and higher data rates, suitable for more demanding applications. The choice between the two depends on specific requirements like range, speed, and device complexity in various IoT and consumer electronics.</p></p>
