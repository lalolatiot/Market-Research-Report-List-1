<p><ul><li>Chlorine-Based Compound Fertilizers</li><li>Sulfur-Based Compound Fertilizers</li><li>Nitro-Based Compound Fertilizers</li><li>Urea-Based Compound Fertilizer</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The NPK fertilizer market comprises several types of compound fertilizers: </p><p>1. **Chlorine-Based Compound Fertilizers** contain potassium chloride, enhancing plant growth but may be unsuitable for chlorine-sensitive crops.  </p><p>2. **Sulfur-Based Compound Fertilizers** frequently include ammonium sulfate, providing both nitrogen and sulfur, beneficial for crops needing these nutrients.  </p><p>3. **Nitro-Based Compound Fertilizers** focus on nitrogen, promoting robust vegetative growth, essential for many crops.  </p><p>4. **Urea-Based Compound Fertilizers** are rich in nitrogen, highly soluble, and widely used due to their effectiveness and cost-efficiency in agriculture.</p></p>
