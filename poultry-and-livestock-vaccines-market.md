<p><p>The poultry and livestock vaccines market comprises two primary types: live and inactivated vaccines. Live vaccines contain weakened forms of pathogens, stimulating a strong immune response without causing disease, making them effective for widespread immunization. In contrast, inactivated vaccines use killed pathogens, offering safety but often requiring booster doses for sustained immunity. Both types are crucial in preventing diseases in livestock and poultry, enhancing animal health, productivity, and food security in the agricultural sector.</p></p>
