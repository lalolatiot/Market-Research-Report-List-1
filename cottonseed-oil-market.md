<p><p>The cottonseed oil market can be divided into two main types: crude cottonseed oil and edible cottonseed oil. Crude cottonseed oil is extracted directly from cottonseeds and undergoes minimal processing, often used in industrial applications or as a feedstock for further refinement. In contrast, edible cottonseed oil is refined to meet food safety standards, making it suitable for cooking and food production. This refined version has a neutral flavor and higher smoke point, appealing to consumers and food manufacturers alike.</p></p>
