<p><p>Semiconductor wafer electrostatic chucks (ESCs) are critical components in semiconductor manufacturing used for holding wafers during processing. The market comprises different types, including Coulomb type and Johnsen-Rahbek (JR) type. Coulomb type ESCs utilize electrostatic forces for wafer adhesion, offering precise control and stability. In contrast, JR type ESCs leverage a combination of electrostatic forces and a dielectric layer to enhance adhesion and reduce particle generation. Both types aim to improve processing efficiency and yield in semiconductor production.</p></p>
