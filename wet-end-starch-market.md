<p><ul><li>Cationic Starch</li><li>Amphoteric Starch</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The wet-end starch market is primarily categorized into cationic and amphoteric starch types, both serving different functions in papermaking. Cationic starch is positively charged, enhancing retention and drainage, while improving strength and printability. It acts as a binder, improving fiber bond strength. Amphoteric starch has both positive and negative charges, providing versatility in applications by improving the water-holding capacity and enhancing fiber interaction. Both types play crucial roles in optimizing the paper's quality and performance.</p></p>
