<p><p>Neurosurgery surgical navigation systems utilize advanced technologies to enhance precision during brain and spine surgeries. The market is primarily segmented into two types: optical and electromagnetic navigation systems. Optical systems use cameras and markers to track instruments' positions in relation to the patient's anatomy, providing high accuracy in real-time. In contrast, electromagnetic systems employ magnetic fields to locate surgical instruments, offering flexibility and ease of use, especially in complex surgical environments. Both systems aim to improve patient outcomes and surgical efficiency.</p></p>
