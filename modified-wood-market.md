<p><p>The Modified Wood market includes Thermally Modified Wood and Chemical Modified Wood. Thermally Modified Wood involves heating wood to high temperatures in a controlled environment, enhancing durability, stability, and resistance to decay without chemicals. Chemical Modified Wood uses chemical treatments, such as acetylation or resin impregnation, to improve moisture resistance, dimensional stability, and longevity. Both types cater to applications like decking, cladding, and furniture, offering eco-friendly alternatives to traditional treated wood while addressing specific performance and sustainability needs.</p></p>
